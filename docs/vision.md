# Vision

Autonomous science will not be unlocked by a single better model. It will be unlocked by
a **reasoning and evaluation layer** that connects scientific questions, experimental
data, literature evidence, model predictions, tool calls, and human judgment into
**reusable, auditable workflows**.

`scientific-agent-lab` explores that layer with one non-negotiable principle:

> A scientific agent must never turn scientific reasoning into unsupported text
> generation. Every output separates **evidence**, **assumptions**, **uncertainty**,
> **missing evidence**, and **recommendations** — and stays human-reviewable.

## Why this framing

The field is moving from *"make a good model"* to *"build the whole scientific
workflow"*: knowledge infrastructure, agents, tool use, lab integration, and closed-loop
discovery. The scarce skill is not another benchmark-topping model — it is the
infrastructure that makes scientific agents **trustworthy**: evidence tracking,
uncertainty, human review, and replayable evaluation.

That is the niche this project claims: **evaluation and evidence governance for scientific
agents**, applied first to materials/chemistry and microscopy, and extensible to any
instrument or domain.
